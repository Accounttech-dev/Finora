The Customer

Our client was an established law firm managing a diverse caseload across multiple practice areas. The firm's billing function was overwhelmed by complex multi-matter billing requirements, inconsistent time entry practices, and high rates of invoice revisions. Senior attorneys were spending valuable time on billing administration rather than client-facing legal work.

The firm required a billing support partner with expertise in legal billing standards, LEDES invoice formats, and e-billing submission platforms. Delays in invoicing were extending revenue realization timelines and creating friction in client relationships. A structured back-office billing team was needed to restore operational discipline.

The Challenges

Complex multi-matter billing, inconsistent time entry practices, and high invoice revision rates were delaying revenue realization and straining both internal resources and client relationships.

  • Multi-matter complexity: Complex billing across diverse practice areas overwhelmed the internal billing team's capacity.
  • Inconsistent time entry: Attorneys applied inconsistent time entry practices, creating review and correction burdens downstream.
  • High invoice rejection rate: An 18% invoice rejection rate from clients generated significant rework and delayed revenue recognition.
  • Billing administration burden: Attorneys spent excessive time on billing administration, reducing capacity for billable legal work.
  • Revenue realization delays: Extended billing cycles delayed revenue realization and created cash flow uncertainty for the firm.
  • E-billing platform complexity: Managing e-billing submissions across multiple client platforms required specialized expertise not available internally.
The Solutions

A specialized legal billing team was assigned to manage time entry review, LEDES invoice preparation, expense verification, and e-billing submissions across the firm's major client accounts.

  • Specialized legal billing team: A team with deep legal billing expertise was assigned to manage the firm's complete billing function.
  • Time entry review: All attorney time entries were reviewed and standardized before invoice preparation to minimize client rejections.
  • LEDES invoice preparation: LEDES-compliant invoices were prepared accurately and submitted on schedule for all applicable clients.
  • Expense verification: All billable expenses were verified against supporting documentation prior to inclusion in any invoice.
  • E-billing submissions: E-billing submissions were managed across all required client platforms with full compliance tracking.
  • Process documentation: Billing procedures were documented to ensure consistency and enable quality control at each processing stage.
The Result

The billing engagement produced dramatic improvements in invoice quality, revenue realization speed, and attorney time efficiency across the entire firm.

  • Invoice rejection rate: Invoice rejection rate reduced from 18% to 2%, dramatically reducing rework and improving client satisfaction.
  • Billing realization rate: Billing realization rate improved by 12 percentage points, increasing effective revenue capture for the firm.
  • Days to invoice: Average days to invoice reduced from 45 to 14 days, accelerating cash flow significantly.
  • Attorney billing admin time: Attorney time spent on billing administration cut by 80%, redirected entirely to billable client work.
The Summary

The engagement transformed the firm's billing function through specialized legal billing expertise and disciplined process management. Invoice rejection rates fell sharply, realization improved, and revenue was recognized significantly faster. Attorneys regained substantial time previously consumed by billing administration tasks.

  • Invoice rejection from 18% down to 2%
  • 12-point realization rate improvement
  • 45 to 14 days to invoice
  • 80% attorney admin time reduction
