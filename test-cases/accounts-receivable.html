The Customer

Our clients were two small-sized companies operating in service-oriented sectors. Both organizations lacked dedicated accounts receivable teams, resulting in increasingly aged receivables and unresolved claims. Cash inflows were inconsistent, creating working capital strain that affected day-to-day operations.

The companies required a reliable AR partner capable of implementing structured follow-up processes, managing disputed claims, and providing real-time visibility into receivables performance. A cost-effective shared service model was identified as the appropriate solution given the scale of both organizations.

The Challenges

The absence of dedicated AR resources led to aging receivables, unresolved disputes, and deteriorating working capital positions at both client organizations.

  • Aging receivables: Receivables aging well beyond standard terms without structured follow-up or escalation protocols in place.
  • Missed follow-ups: No systematic follow-up schedule existed for outstanding invoices, allowing debtors to delay payment indefinitely.
  • Unresolved claims: Disputed claims accumulated without resolution, creating write-off risk and cash flow uncertainty.
  • Working capital impact: Delayed collections directly constrained working capital and limited operational investment capacity.
  • Reporting limitations: No real-time AR reporting existed, preventing management from understanding the true receivables position.
  • Bad debt exposure: Extended aging without intervention increased the probability of bad debt write-offs significantly.
The Solutions

A shared AR team was deployed to serve both clients, establishing structured follow-up schedules, dispute resolution workflows, and real-time AR dashboards for management visibility.

  • Shared AR team model: A cost-effective shared team structure served both clients with dedicated attention to each account portfolio.
  • Structured follow-up schedule: A systematic follow-up cadence was established for all outstanding invoices across aging brackets.
  • Dispute resolution workflow: A formal dispute resolution process was implemented to resolve claims efficiently and protect revenue.
  • Real-time AR dashboards: Management dashboards provided live visibility into receivables performance, aging, and collection trends.
  • Escalation protocols: Clear escalation pathways were defined for high-value or persistently overdue accounts.
  • Bad debt prevention: Proactive follow-up and early dispute resolution significantly reduced the risk of uncollectable balances.
The Result

The shared AR engagement produced substantial improvements in collection speed, claims resolution, and combined cost efficiency for both client organizations.

  • Collection period reduction: Average collection period reduced by 22 days across both client portfolios.
  • Claims resolution rate: Claims resolution rate improved to 94%, protecting revenue and reducing write-off risk.
  • Bad debt reduction: Bad debt write-offs reduced by 60%, directly improving the financial stability of both organizations.
  • Combined cost savings: Combined annual cost savings of $180K achieved through the shared service delivery model.
The Summary

The shared AR engagement delivered structured collection processes and dispute resolution capability to two organizations previously lacking dedicated receivables management. Collection periods shortened dramatically, claims resolution improved, and bad debt exposure declined significantly. The shared model provided enterprise-quality AR services at a cost appropriate for smaller organizations.

  • 22-day collection period reduction
  • 94% claims resolution rate
  • 60% bad debt reduction
  • $180K combined annual savings
