The Customer

Our client was a leading US-based distributor of industrial piping systems serving a broad customer base across multiple industrial sectors. Expanding business volumes and tighter payment cycles placed significant pressure on the accounts payable and receivable functions. Internal finance teams were unable to manage the growing workload without impacting service quality.

The client operated an extensive vendor and customer network requiring consistent financial management. Manual invoice processing and scattered communications created recurring operational challenges. Cash flow visibility was limited, and supplier relationships were being strained by payment inconsistencies.

The Challenges

Manual processes, disconnected communications, and insufficient capacity created bottlenecks across AP and AR functions, impacting cash flow and vendor relationships at multiple levels.

  • Manual invoice processing: Invoice processing relied entirely on manual workflows, creating delays and error-prone outcomes.
  • Vendor communications: Scattered vendor communications led to duplicate payments, missed credits, and dispute escalations.
  • Delayed receivables: Delayed receivables created cash flow bottlenecks and constrained working capital availability.
  • Vendor relationship strain: Payment inconsistencies damaged vendor relationships critical to supply chain continuity.
  • Reconciliation backlog: Unreconciled AP and AR items accumulated, obscuring the true financial position of the business.
  • Month-end close delays: AP and AR backlogs consistently delayed month-end close activities and financial reporting.
The Solutions

Standardized AP and AR workflows were implemented along with automated invoice matching and dedicated account managers for key vendor and customer relationships across the organization.

  • Standardized workflows: End-to-end AP and AR workflows were standardized with clear ownership and defined processing steps.
  • Automated invoice matching: Invoice matching automation reduced manual touchpoints and eliminated duplicate payment risk.
  • Dedicated account management: Key vendor and customer accounts received dedicated managers to maintain relationship quality.
  • Dispute resolution framework: A structured dispute resolution process reduced open disputes and accelerated payment resolution.
  • Aging review cadence: Weekly aging reviews ensured timely follow-up on outstanding receivables and overdue payables.
  • Reporting visibility: Real-time AP and AR dashboards provided management with accurate cash flow visibility.
The Result

The standardized AP and AR operations produced significant improvements in processing speed, cash flow management, and vendor relationship stability across the organization.

  • Invoice processing time: Invoice processing time cut by 50%, accelerating payment cycles across the vendor network.
  • Days Sales Outstanding: DSO reduced by 18 days, improving working capital and cash flow predictability.
  • Vendor payment disputes: Vendor payment disputes reduced by 70%, strengthening key supply chain relationships.
  • Month-end close: Month-end close accelerated by 4 days, enabling earlier financial reporting to leadership.
The Summary

The engagement transformed AP and AR operations through workflow standardization, automation, and dedicated account management. Processing speed, cash flow visibility, and vendor relationship quality all improved substantially. The accelerated month-end close supported more timely and accurate financial reporting.

  • 50% faster invoice processing
  • 18-day DSO reduction
  • 70% fewer vendor disputes
  • 4-day close acceleration
