The Customer

Our client was a growing logistics company managing a high volume of supplier transactions across multiple distribution channels. The organization processed significant quantities of transaction documents daily, including invoices, proofs of delivery, and bills of lading. The accounts payable function lacked the structured processes required to handle this volume efficiently.

Supplier payment obligations were critical to maintaining supply chain continuity. Document processing backlogs had accumulated over several months, creating reconciliation challenges and damaging vendor relationships. The finance team required immediate structural intervention to restore operational stability.

The Challenges

Unstructured document processing workflows and accumulated backlogs created significant cash flow and vendor relationship challenges across the entire AP function.

  • Document processing backlog: Months of unprocessed transaction documents created unresolvable reconciliation challenges for the finance team.
  • Inconsistent workflows: No standardized intake or validation process existed for the various document types received daily.
  • Supplier payment delays: Payment delays strained key vendor relationships critical to supply chain operations.
  • Audit trail gaps: Insufficient documentation trails created compliance exposure during internal and external financial reviews.
  • Cash flow opacity: Unprocessed payables obscured the true cash flow position, complicating treasury management decisions.
  • High processing cost: Manual, ad-hoc document handling resulted in excessive per-document processing costs.
The Solutions

A dedicated AP team was deployed to design an end-to-end document intake, validation, and processing system with custom checklists and service level agreements for each document type.

  • End-to-end document system: A complete document intake, validation, and processing system was designed and implemented from the ground up.
  • Standardized checklists: Custom processing checklists were created for each transaction document type to ensure consistent handling.
  • SLA establishment: Defined service level agreements for each document category ensured predictable processing timelines.
  • Backlog clearance: The accumulated document processing backlog was systematically cleared within 30 days of engagement commencement.
  • Audit trail compliance: A complete and traceable audit trail was established for all processed documents going forward.
  • Payment standardization: Supplier payment cycles were standardized to net-15 terms across all vendor categories.
The Result

The AP restructuring delivered rapid backlog resolution, standardized payment cycles, and significant cost improvements across all document processing categories.

  • Backlog cleared: Document processing backlog cleared within 30 days, restoring operational stability across the AP function.
  • Payment cycle standardization: Supplier payment cycle standardized to net-15, strengthening vendor relationships and supply chain reliability.
  • 100% audit trail compliance: Complete document audit trail compliance achieved, eliminating all compliance exposure.
  • Processing cost reduction: Processing cost per document reduced by 42%, significantly improving overall AP operational efficiency.
The Summary

The engagement restructured the client's accounts payable function through a purpose-built document processing system. The backlog was cleared rapidly, supplier payment cycles standardized, and processing costs reduced substantially. Full audit trail compliance provided ongoing protection during financial reviews.

  • 30-day backlog clearance
  • Net-15 payment cycle standardized
  • 100% audit trail compliance
  • 42% cost per document reduction
